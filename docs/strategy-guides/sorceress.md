# Sorceress Strategy Source

Last updated: March 28, 2026.

## Strategy Snapshot

Sorceress should feel like the clearest spell-engine class. The class should split between fire payoff, cold denial, and lightning tempo. It should care more than most classes about draw texture, hand size, energy, and sequencing around vulnerable setup windows.

## Live Tracked Archetypes

| Archetype | Core tree | Support tree | Preferred weapons | Main ask | Main failure mode |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cold Control | Cold | Lightning | Staff | Deny the enemy long enough to safely convert to lethal | Too much control, not enough kill pressure |
| Fire Burst | Fire | Lightning | Staff | Build toward explosive payoff turns | Dies before payoff turns stabilize |

## Archetype Briefs

### Cold Control

- Identity: slow or freeze the fight down, then win on safe terms
- Wants: denial, survival, and enough finishing pressure that control actually matters
- Boss plan: avoid over-drafting safety and under-drafting payoff
- Guide note: this should be taught as the safest but most precision-dependent lane

### Fire Burst

- Identity: delayed explosive turns, burn scaling, and meaningful setup windows
- Wants: enough protection and consistency to reach the powerful turns
- Boss plan: survive the opener and avoid dying with payoff stuck in hand
- Guide note: frame this as the classic Sorceress power lane

### Lightning Tempo

- Identity: acceleration, draw, energy, and high-variance swing turns
- Wants: the best hand texture support in the roster, plus a staff line that keeps tempo high
- Boss plan: manage volatility instead of assuming every swing turn appears on time
- Guide note: explain that this lane is strong when the player plans around randomness instead of ignoring it

## Run-Shaping Signals

- early cold control rewards and safe staff progress point toward `Cold Control`
- early fire payoff and burn scaling point toward `Fire Burst`
- early draw or energy tempo with lightning support points toward `Lightning Tempo`

## Future Guide Emphasis

- make opening draw and hand texture central to the guide
- explain why Sorceress can still lose early if the deck is too greedy
- show the difference between “powerful spell cards” and a real spell engine
