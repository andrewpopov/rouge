# Assassin Strategy Source

Last updated: March 28, 2026.

## Strategy Snapshot

Assassin should feel like the most sequencing-sensitive class. Its best runs should depend on whether the player is building for burst windows, trap setup, or flexible shadow tempo. It should reward planning turns ahead more than most classes.

## Archetype Briefs

### Martial Burst

- Identity: build charge or setup, then cash it in on a planned burst turn
- Wants: burst engines, survival tools, and a clean deck that finds payoff cards often
- Boss plan: respect telegraphs and keep enough guard or defense to survive the setup window
- Guide note: this should read like the “hardest to pilot, highest payoff” lane

### Shadow Tempo

- Identity: evasion, flexibility, medium-value turns, and tactical adaptation
- Wants: cards that preserve tempo while smoothing awkward hands
- Boss plan: avoid becoming too fair; the deck still needs a way to end a fight
- Guide note: teach this as the Assassin lane that wins by choice density and clean sequencing

### Trap Field

- Identity: delayed area pressure and enemy-wave planning
- Wants: enough time, enough deck control, and enough support to survive until the field matters
- Boss plan: add durable setup and avoid overloading on slow pieces
- Guide note: frame this as the lane that cares most about draw order and encounter shape

## Run-Shaping Signals

- early martial investment plus burst attack rewards should lock toward `Martial Burst`
- shadow support or evasion-heavy rewards should signal `Shadow Tempo`
- repeated delayed AoE or setup rewards should indicate `Trap Field`

## Future Guide Emphasis

- explain why Assassin decks need a real ratio of setup to payoff
- warn players against overdrafting cool trap cards without enough survival
- highlight that Assassin is one of the classes most likely to care about hand texture and opening draw
