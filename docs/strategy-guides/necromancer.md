# Necromancer Strategy Source

Last updated: March 28, 2026.

## Strategy Snapshot

Necromancer should feel like the most deliberate setup class in the roster. It should win either by preparing a bone burst finish, controlling the enemy with curses, or scaling into a summon-based engine. It should not feel like generic caster goodstuff.

## Archetype Briefs

### Curse Control

- Identity: weaken the enemy, buy time, and win through cleaner long fights
- Wants: control density, survivability, and enough finishing pressure to punish stalled enemies
- Boss plan: turn control into a real answer, not just delayed death
- Guide note: this should be framed as the most tactical Necromancer lane

### Bone Burst

- Identity: control first, then explode with bone payoff
- Wants: curses, survivability, and a wand setup that reinforces the burst lane
- Boss plan: draft enough setup consistency that the payoff actually appears in time
- Guide note: treat this as the signature “setup then detonate” Necromancer strategy

### Summon Swarm

- Identity: scale the fight around minion presence and support sequencing
- Wants: summon density, support cards that keep the board alive, and enough backup damage
- Boss plan: survive the vulnerable setup turns and avoid overly passive decks
- Guide note: this is the most engine-like Necromancer lane and should be explained that way

## Run-Shaping Signals

- early curse-heavy picks point toward `Curse Control`
- early bone payoff or wand scaling points toward `Bone Burst`
- early summon density and engine support point toward `Summon Swarm`

## Future Guide Emphasis

- explain that control alone is not a win condition
- make wand progression and runeword goals a major part of the guide
- highlight that Necromancer should care strongly about hand texture and setup timing
