# Amazon Strategy Source

Last updated: March 28, 2026.

## Strategy Snapshot

Amazon should feel like a precision class whose runs split between ranged consistency, javelin tempo bursts, and passive-enabled efficiency. Early choices should make it clear whether the run is becoming a ranged engine, an all-in spear or javelin pressure deck, or a tempo shell that wins by efficiency and repeated attacks.

## Archetype Briefs

### Bow Volley

- Identity: repeated ranged attacks, cleaner draw texture, and reliable pressure over explosive spikes
- Wants: bow or crossbow progression, chill or freeze support, and enough draw quality to keep pressure turns frequent
- Boss plan: survive long enough to line up repeated ranged payoff turns instead of trying to one-cycle bosses
- Guide note: present this as the “most stable” Amazon lane

### Javelin Storm

- Identity: faster and riskier Amazon lane built around aggressive tempo swings
- Wants: javelin or spear progression, shock-style pressure, and support that keeps the hero alive during committed attack turns
- Boss plan: draft enough guard or tempo support that burst turns do not turn into suicide
- Guide note: present this as the lane with the highest upside and the highest punish window

### Passive Tempo

- Identity: turn-by-turn efficiency, dodges, crit-style tempo, and mercenary support
- Wants: passive investment plus a weapon that keeps repeated attacks efficient
- Boss plan: make sure the deck still has a real boss answer, not only clean small-fight tempo
- Guide note: present this as the “play clean and scale smart” lane

## Run-Shaping Signals

- Early Bow investment plus a bow-family weapon should push the run toward `Bow Volley`
- Early Jab or Charged Strike style pressure plus a javelin-family weapon should push toward `Javelin Storm`
- Early passive support cards without a locked weapon lane should point toward `Passive Tempo`

## Future Guide Emphasis

- explain when to commit to bows versus javelins
- explain that passive support is not a standalone win condition unless the deck has real damage scaling
- teach players to evaluate whether the run is consistency-first or burst-first by late Act II
