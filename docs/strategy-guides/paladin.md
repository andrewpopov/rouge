# Paladin Strategy Source

Last updated: March 28, 2026.

## Strategy Snapshot

Paladin should feel like a class defined by how it turns auras into fight-winning structure. Its runs should branch between repeated combat attacks, defensive inevitability, and aura-driven scaling. The class should feel safe when drafted well, but punish decks that never build a real payoff.

## Archetype Briefs

### Combat Zeal

- Identity: attack repeatedly, stay upright, and keep the pressure on
- Wants: reliable melee weapons and aura support that lets attacks stay safe
- Boss plan: survive telegraphed spikes while keeping repeated attack value high
- Guide note: this is the most intuitive Paladin lane and should teach the basic class loop

### Defensive Anchor

- Identity: long-fight control through survivability and aura-backed stability
- Wants: defensive scaling plus enough payoff to punish long fights
- Boss plan: answer burst windows and win the attrition war
- Guide note: emphasize that “tanky” is not enough without a real closing plan

### Offensive Aura

- Identity: build-around scaling that turns routine attacks into major turns
- Wants: aura density, good melee attacks, and enough consistency to keep the engine assembled
- Boss plan: make the aura engine matter before the boss overwhelms the deck
- Guide note: position this as Paladin’s most synergy-sensitive lane

## Run-Shaping Signals

- early combat investment plus a good melee weapon suggests `Combat Zeal`
- early aura rewards without enough attacks should warn the player to choose a lane soon
- repeated defensive aura value points toward `Defensive Anchor`

## Future Guide Emphasis

- explain how Paladin balances attack cadence versus setup
- make clear that offensive auras need actual attack density
- highlight that Paladin success depends on surviving and converting, not just staying alive
