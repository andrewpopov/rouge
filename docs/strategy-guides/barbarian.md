# Barbarian Strategy Source

Last updated: March 28, 2026.

## Strategy Snapshot

Barbarian should feel like a frontline class whose lanes differ by how it converts weapon pressure into survivable fights. It should not collapse into “attack every turn.” The interesting question is whether the run is raw combat pressure, durable mastery scaling, or timing-based warcry tempo.

## Archetype Briefs

### Combat Pressure

- Identity: proactive frontline damage with just enough defense to keep swinging
- Wants: strong weapon upgrades, guard-positive attacks, and enough tempo to keep fights short
- Boss plan: draft for survival during telegraphed burst turns, not only for damage
- Guide note: this is the default-feeling Barbarian lane, but it should still require restraint

### Mastery Frontline

- Identity: reliable weapon scaling and sturdy draw patterns
- Wants: good weapon family progression and fewer dead support cards
- Boss plan: outlast awkward hands and keep pressure consistent
- Guide note: position this as the most stable Barbarian path

### Warcry Tempo

- Identity: buffs, taunts, guard, and party control
- Wants: sequencing tools and mercenary support that make timing matter
- Boss plan: survive long enough for buff windows to outperform raw stat racing
- Guide note: this is the most “deckbuilder” Barbarian lane and should be framed that way

## Run-Shaping Signals

- early combat-tree picks and strong melee weapons suggest `Combat Pressure`
- mastery investment plus stable weapon progression suggests `Mastery Frontline`
- repeated guard, buff, or mercenary support picks suggest `Warcry Tempo`

## Future Guide Emphasis

- teach players that Barbarian still needs a boss plan, not just damage
- explain why weapon quality matters more here than on most classes
- show the difference between “pressure” and “overextending”
