# Druid Strategy Source

Last updated: March 28, 2026.

## Strategy Snapshot

Druid should feel like the class with the widest texture change between archetypes. Elemental should be a spell-sequencing deck, shape shifting should be a durable bruiser plan, and summoning should be a scaling engine that wins by stabilizing the fight.

## Archetype Briefs

### Elemental Storm

- Identity: delayed but powerful spell turns
- Wants: typed-damage support, enough survival, and a deck that reaches its good turns on time
- Boss plan: survive the early cycles and then convert spells into a real finishing sequence
- Guide note: teach this as the most payoff-driven Druid lane

### Shifter Bruiser

- Identity: resilient melee play with attack chaining and better tolerance for awkward draws
- Wants: durable melee weapons and support that keeps the run from turning into simple stat racing
- Boss plan: draft enough reach to avoid stalling once bosses stabilize
- Guide note: frame this as the “honest but sturdy” Druid lane

### Summoner Engine

- Identity: survive, establish board presence, then let the engine carry long fights
- Wants: summon density, support that protects setup turns, and enough payoff once the board is online
- Boss plan: avoid slow hands that never actually take control
- Guide note: this is the lane that should most clearly feel like an engine build

## Run-Shaping Signals

- early elemental investment and staff progress point toward `Elemental Storm`
- early melee weapon progression plus shape-shifting picks point toward `Shifter Bruiser`
- early summon support and engine rewards point toward `Summoner Engine`

## Future Guide Emphasis

- explain how Druid shifts from “survive” to “convert”
- distinguish summon support from true summon payoff
- show players when a staff plan is real and when a mace fallback is the safer lane
